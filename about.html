Yvonne is the president after the re-activation of the RSG Germany, a PhD student for computational biology, doing me2sports and involved in volunteer work.

Her PhD work focuses on the best drug predictions for acute leukemia as well as other projects to evaluate PD and Alzheimer. The former work was about antibiotic resistances, bacteria subtyping of hospital strains and the differences in early- and elderly-onset prostate cancer.

Niko is Greek but his life as a Bioinformatician started with his studies in Munich. He is currently pursuing his PhD in Computational Biology at the lab of Johannes Soeding (Max Planck Institute for Biophysical Chemistry, Göttingen), where he is developing a noise model for single-cell RNA sequencing data. Anubhav is a Masters student in Molecular Biology at the Max Planck Institute for Biophysical Chemistry, Göttingen.

He is interested in statistical learning and application of Artificial Intelligence to solve problems in health sectors.

Currently, he is applying different Statistical methosds for comprehensive discory of cis and trans eQTLs in the human genome. He is a strong believer in opportunities that the student associations offer to connect to people, learn ideas and empower budding computational biologists.

Neetika Nath
Acquisition Member

Institute for Bioinformatics, University Medicine Greifswald

Read more


This is me, Neetika Nath. I am a Bioinformatician.

I say this because my major during my undergraduate, postgraduate and PhD studies was Bioinformatics. After completing my PhD successfully at the University of St. Andrews, Scotland, where I developed a machine learning pipeline to identify enzyme function, I moved to University Medicine Greifswald, Germany. Here I am analyzing NGS data to understand the underlying mechanism of the effect of radiation on human skin at a higher dosage. My interest is to apply my knowledge to solve the problem in medical and health area. In my free time I enjoy doing sports, swimming, strong Zumba, and meeting with friends. I love nature that's why I always hope for a bright sunny day so that I can go out to hike.

Tommaso Andreani
Newsletter Editor

Institute of Molecular Biology, Johannes Gutenberg University Mainz

Read more


Tommaso is a Doctoral candidate at the Institute of Molecular Biology in Mainz at the Computational Biology, DNA Demethylation and Reprogramming laboratories under the supervision of Miguel Andrade and Christof Niehrs. He is using computational and statistical approaches to study the DNA demethylation and repair pathway in mouse ES cells and he is developing a method able to distinguish "hot" vs "stochastically variable" regions in an isogenic population of mammalian cells using ChIP experiments.
